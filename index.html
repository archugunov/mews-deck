The Problem

Hotels pay for upsells they never switch on

  • The dark upsell — the hotel sets up "Breakfast — €15", but it ships switched off across every stay phase — guests never see it. No error, no alert. It earns €0.
  • Not a product failure — a surfacing one — the upsell converts the moment it's shown; the hotel just never shows it.
  • The gap is systemic — only 1 in 3 software buyers ever adopts what they bought (research Mews cites), and Mews's own capabilities show the same pattern (right).

Definition

Who feels it — and why now

  • Segment — independent hotels, where the GM is also the revenue manager. Chains have RMS teams that catch a dark upsell; independents don't.
  • Persona — the GM / Revenue Manager first (they hold the permission to enable); then front-desk staff — read-only, flagging opportunities up.
  • Why now — margins are tight (labour cost per occupied room up ~13% in 2025, US), OTAs take most of an independent's bookings, and Cloudbeds already ships an "Opportunities" tab.
The goal Grow upsell revenue per property — expansion inside the installed base, not new logos.
Evidence signal

+70% more upsell revenue per check-in once the kiosk is on, across the ~15,000 hotels already on the platform (Mews's own data).

The Solution

An "Opportunities" section on the Operations Dashboard

In scope
  • Two item types — dark and under-promoted upsells.
  • Grouped by service — the operator's own catalogue (e.g. Minibar = 5 products), not a flat product list.
  • Detect them, price the revenue at stake, and deep-link the GM to switch it on — human in the loop.
Out of scope
  • Live-earning receipts (fast-follow) · never-set-up upsells (V2) · other Mews capabilities (V3).

Deterministic core

Detection, ranking, and every number are pure rules — no model on the money path. The figures are auditable, never hallucinated.

AI does the two jobs rules can't

Separating a genuine upsell from a point-of-sale line-item, and writing an explanation a template can't. AI does only those two, and states only the numbers the rules computed — never its own.

Every AI output is checked in code

Three deterministic checks: grounding rejects any number not in the data; claim-check catches a false claim about a real one; a gold-set fails the build if the classifier buries a real upsell.

Metrics

  • North Star% of properties with revenue-generating upsells

The caveat
Revenue-generating isn't the same as incremental
  • Some guests would have bought at the desk anyway — channel-shift, not created revenue.
  • The honest read is a matched holdout; V1 measures before/after per property and names the gap.
Adoption & iteration

Drive adoption, then tune on the data

Drive adoption
  • Native on the dashboard home — in view at every login, no new tool to learn.
  • Email digest — reaches the absent GM who isn't a daily user.
  • Front-desk visibility — sees opportunities read-only, flags them to the manager who can act.
Improve on data & feedback

Two signals to tune on — and the one outcome that would make us pivot.

  • Weak leading indicators (low activation, low acceptance) → re-rank and tighten relevance.
  • Distrust (high reversal, high mute) → tighten grounding, price more conservatively.
  • Rare in the real base → re-point the engine at never-set-up upsells & other dormant capabilities.
